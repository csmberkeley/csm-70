\textbf{Conditional Expectation}: We can use the joint distribution to determine the conditional expectation of $Y$ given $X$:  
\[\E[Y | X = x] = \sum_y y \cdot \P[Y = y | X = x] = \sum_y y \cdot 
\frac{\P[X = x, Y = y]}{\P[X = x]}\]
Some important properties of conditional expectation include: 
\begin{equation}
\begin{split}
\E (a | Y) &= a \\ \nonumber
\E (aX + bZ | Y) &= a \cdot \E(X | Y) + b \cdot \E(Z | Y) \\
%\E(X | Y) &\geq 0 \text{ if } X \geq 0 \\
\E(X | Y) &= \E(X) \text{ if } X, Y \text{ independent} \\
%\E( \E(X | Y)) &= \E(X)
\end{split}
\end{equation}
\end{minipage}}
